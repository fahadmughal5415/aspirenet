How to Get More Customers Using Local SEO in 2026

AspireNet Team June 2026 11 min read

Local SEO is the practice of optimizing your online presence so your business appears when people in your area search for what you offer. For most small businesses, it is the single highest-return marketing activity available. This guide gives you a practical action plan you can start using today.

What is Local SEO and Why Does It Matter

When someone searches for "electrician near me" or "best pizza in Austin," Google shows local results based on the person's location. These results include the Google Map Pack (three local businesses shown with a map) and organic search results below.

Appearing in these results puts your business directly in front of people who are ready to buy right now. Unlike social media marketing where you push ads to people who may not need your service, local SEO connects you with people actively searching for exactly what you offer.

Research consistently shows that 72% of people who search for a local business visit a store within five miles within 24 hours. The businesses that appear at the top of local search capture a disproportionate share of this ready-to-buy audience.

Step 1: Set Up and Optimize Your Google Business Profile

Your Google Business Profile is free and is the most important local SEO asset you have. If you have not claimed your listing yet, go to business.google.com and claim it now.

Once claimed, optimize every section:

  • Choose the most specific primary category (for example "Italian Restaurant" not just "Restaurant")
  • Write a complete description that naturally includes your main services and city name
  • Add every service you offer with descriptions and prices where applicable
  • Upload at least 20 photos including your storefront, interior, team, and work samples
  • Set accurate hours including holiday hours
  • Enable messaging so customers can contact you directly from Google
  • Post weekly updates about offers, events, or new services

Step 2: Target Local Keywords on Your Website

Your website needs to tell Google what services you offer and where you serve customers. This sounds simple, but many small business websites are vague about location and services.

Include your city and neighborhood naturally in your page titles, headings, and content. For example, instead of "We offer plumbing services," write "We offer emergency plumbing services to homeowners throughout Richmond, VA and surrounding areas including Henrico County."

Create separate pages for your most important service categories rather than listing everything on one page. A plumbing company might have separate pages for "Emergency Plumbing Richmond VA," "Drain Cleaning Richmond VA," and "Water Heater Installation Richmond VA."

Step 3: Make Sure Your Website is Fast and Mobile-Friendly

Over 60% of local searches happen on mobile devices. If your website is slow or difficult to use on a phone, people will leave immediately and call your competitor. Google also penalizes slow, mobile-unfriendly websites in local search rankings.

Test your site at Google's Mobile-Friendly Test and PageSpeed Insights. Both are free. Address any issues flagged, especially image compression and hosting speed.

A well-optimized Google Business Profile combined with a fast, mobile-friendly website is enough for many local businesses to rank in the top three Google Maps results within 60 to 90 days.

Step 4: Build Local Citations

Submit your business to the major local directories consistently. Your business name, address, and phone number must be identical across every directory. Even small differences can confuse Google's algorithms.

Start with these free, high-authority directories:

  • Yelp
  • Yellow Pages
  • Bing Places for Business
  • Apple Maps
  • Facebook Business Page
  • Better Business Bureau
  • Your local Chamber of Commerce

Step 5: Get More Google Reviews Systematically

Reviews are one of the strongest signals in local search rankings. Businesses with more recent, positive reviews consistently rank higher and convert more visitors into customers.

The most effective way to get more reviews is to simply ask. After every successful job or transaction, send a message to the customer with a direct link to your Google review page. Most satisfied customers are happy to leave a review when asked directly and given an easy way to do it.

Respond to every review within 24 hours. For positive reviews, thank the customer and mention the service they used. For negative reviews, apologize professionally, explain what you will do to resolve the issue, and take the conversation offline. How you respond to negative reviews matters as much as the review itself.

Step 6: Stay Consistent Over Time

Local SEO is not a one-time project. Google rewards businesses that stay active. Post on your Google Business Profile at least once a week, respond to all reviews promptly, and update your hours and services whenever they change.

Track your results monthly using Google Business Profile insights, which shows you how many people found your profile, what they searched for, and whether they called, visited your website, or asked for directions. Use this data to identify which services are driving the most searches and create more content around those topics.
