How to Choose the Best Web Design Agency for Your Business

AspireNet Team June 2025 9 min read

Choosing the wrong web design agency can cost you significant time and money. With thousands of agencies and freelancers available, it can be difficult to know who to trust. Here are ten questions that will help you identify the right partner for your project.

1. Review Their Portfolio Carefully

A reputable agency will have a strong portfolio of completed projects. Look for websites similar to what you need in terms of industry, complexity, and style. If the agency primarily builds eCommerce sites and you need a service business website, they may not be the best fit.

Ask to see live websites, not just screenshots. A live site lets you test speed, mobile responsiveness, and the overall user experience directly.

2. Check Their Platform Specialization

Different agencies specialize in different platforms. An agency that primarily builds Webflow sites may not be the best choice for a WooCommerce store. Ask which platforms they work with most often and why they recommend a specific platform for your project.

Be cautious of agencies that push you toward expensive custom development when a platform like WordPress or Shopify would serve your needs at a fraction of the cost.

3. Do They Include SEO in Their Work

A beautiful website that no one can find is useless. Ask specifically whether the agency includes on-page SEO setup as part of their service. This should include proper title tags, meta descriptions, image optimization, XML sitemap submission, and Google Search Console setup.

If an agency says SEO is a completely separate service and charges extra for basic optimization, that is a warning sign. Fundamental SEO should be part of every professional web build.

4. Assess Their Communication Style

Before signing any contract, pay attention to how the agency communicates during the initial consultation. Do they respond promptly? Do they listen to your goals before pitching solutions? Do they explain things clearly without overwhelming technical jargon?

The way an agency communicates before you become a client is usually how they communicate during the project. If getting a response takes days during the sales process, expect the same during development.

5. Ask About Realistic Timelines

Be cautious of any agency that promises to build a professional website in 24 to 48 hours. A properly built website requires planning, design, development, and testing. Rushing these stages leads to poor quality work.

A basic business website should take 7 to 14 days. A complex site with custom features should take 3 to 8 weeks. Ask for a written timeline and milestones so you can track progress.

6. Understand Exactly What You Are Paying For

Get a detailed written proposal that breaks down exactly what is included in the price. This should specify the number of pages, features, revisions, hosting setup, and any ongoing costs. Vague quotes like "a website for $X" often lead to scope creep and unexpected charges.

Always get a written contract before any work begins. It should specify the scope of work, payment schedule, revision policy, timeline, and what happens if the project is delayed on either side.

7. Ask About Post-Launch Support

What happens after your website goes live? Bugs and issues often surface in the days following a launch. Ask whether the agency provides a support period after delivery and how long it lasts.

Also ask about ongoing maintenance. WordPress sites need regular updates to stay secure. Clarify whether the agency offers maintenance packages and what they include.

8. Confirm Who Owns the Website

This is critical. Some agencies retain ownership of website code and design, making you dependent on them for any future changes. Make sure the contract clearly states that you own all website files, designs, and content after final payment.

9. Check Independent Reviews

Look for reviews on Google, Clutch, or LinkedIn rather than relying solely on testimonials on the agency's own website. Ask the agency for references from past clients and actually contact those clients to ask about their experience.

10. Watch for These Red Flags

Be cautious if an agency refuses to provide references, cannot explain their pricing clearly, promises first-page Google rankings without explaining their strategy, requires full payment upfront, or pressures you to decide immediately.
